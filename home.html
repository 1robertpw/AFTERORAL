About Me

I'm eager to contribute to the baseball industry through data-driven insights and biomechanical research. By combining my education in information science with experience as a competitive pitcher, I aim to bridge the gap between analytics and on-field performance. After pursuing professional baseball in 2026, I plan to begin a PhD program in Fall 2027, integrating both my research interests and practical playing experience.

Research Interests

  • Overhead Throwing Mechanics: Thoracic musculature function, biomechanical sequencing, and strength in overhead throwing athletes
  • Patellar Tendon/PFJ: Tendinopathy mechanisms and testing innovative research protocols for rehabilitation
  • Lower Body Mechanics: Front-leg stabilization, ground reaction forces, and associated energy/heat production during dynamic movements
